# Story Enhancement Notes

Checkpoint date: 2026-06-30

## Creative Target

The project should feel like grounded psychological survival horror: intimate, slow, tense, and character-led. The player should fear the hotel, but the story should hurt because Saqer is forced to reconsider the simple story he told himself about his father's disappearance.

## High-Level Influences

- Domestic horror pressure: a familiar family space becomes threatening because love, debt, and control are mixed together.
- Symbolic spaces: the lobby, CCTV room, basement bank, family table, Room 000, and Eye Tower should all accuse Saqer in different ways.
- Human monsters before literal monsters: the Bellboy, Qareen, Zarqa, Bride, Father, and Mother each reflect a part of Saqer's wound.
- Restraint matters: the strongest ending should require empathy, self-knowledge, low denial, and low violence.

This is an original story direction for **The Last Check-In: Eyes of Al-Yamama**. It should borrow only broad survival-horror principles, never protected plot, character, scene, dialogue, or exact visual identity from other games.

## Character Depth Added

- Saqer is not just "the player"; he is an eighteen-year-old who has survived by simplifying grief into blame.
- Nasser, the father, protected Saqer by sacrificing memories, but the sacrifice damaged the relationship it was meant to save.
- Alya, the mother, hid practical truths because survival came before explanation.
- Zarqa is not the hotel's eye; she is a captive witness who needs the future to stop being a cage.
- The Qareen is Saqer's learned survival cruelty, not a random shadow monster.
- Al-Mudawwin is the logic of debt made supernatural: it cannot understand love unless love has a price.
