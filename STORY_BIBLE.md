# STORY BIBLE
# THE LAST CHECK-IN: EYES OF AL-YAMAMA
## آخر تسجيل دخول: عيون اليمامة

---

## 1. Core Pitch

**A cursed desert resort uses the stolen eyes of Zarqa Al-Yamama to predict every guest’s death. On his 18th birthday, Saqer receives a check-in receipt from the abandoned resort that once swallowed his father. The hotel has waited eighteen years to collect him. But Saqer is the first guest whose future cannot be seen.**

---

## 2. Main Character

### Saqer
- **Age:** 18
- **Symbol:** Falcon / the unseen path
- **Fear:** That his life was never his own
- **Emotional wound:** He grew up believing his father abandoned him
- **Truth:** His father stayed trapped in the hotel for eighteen years to delay Saqer’s collection
- **Core question:** If Saqer’s life was purchased before he could choose, does he still own himself?

### Why 18 Matters
The hotel could not legally/spiritually collect Saqer as a child. When Saqer turns 18, the contract matures.

The hotel’s first line:

> “Congratulations, Mr. Saqer. You are now old enough to pay.”

---

## 3. Setting

### Qasr Al-Mirath Resort
**Arabic:** قصر الميراث  
**Meaning:** Palace of Inheritance

A closed luxury desert resort built above the ruins of an ancient buried watchtower and the Ledger Well.

The resort is beautiful from a distance:
- Marble lobby
- Gold signs
- Desert-view suites
- Wedding halls
- Private bank floor
- CCTV control center
- Underground vaults

But inside, it is a spiritual machine:
- Rooms harvest memories
- Corridors record footsteps
- Cameras are cursed eyes
- Receipts are contracts
- Elevators move through emotions, not floors

---

## 4. Mythic Foundation

### Zarqa Al-Yamama
A legendary Arab woman known for impossible sight. In this fictional horror version, Zarqa was cursed to see disasters before they happened. Every future she saw became harder to escape.

After her death, Al-Mudawwin stole her eyes and chained her sight to the buried tower. Centuries later, the resort founder connected her vision to CCTV.

**Core reveal:**

> The CCTV is not recording Saqer. Zarqa is watching him.

Zarqa is not the villain. She is a prisoner.

---

## 5. Main Entity

### Al-Mudawwin
**Arabic:** المدوّن  
**Meaning:** The One Who Records

An ancient jinn/entity that believes reality is debt.

Rules:
- Nothing is free.
- Everything living owes something.
- Every step is a signature.
- Every memory can be deposited.
- Every future can be collected.

It appears through:
- Receipts
- Hotel screens
- ATMs
- CCTV feeds
- Guest cards
- Elevator panels
- Room phones
- Moving Arabic calligraphy

Signature lines:
> “You are not haunted. You are overdue.”  
> “Correction required.”  
> “Guest has attempted unauthorized future.”  
> “Nothing is forgotten. Everything is owed.”

---

## 6. Main Stalker

### The Bellboy
The hotel’s collector.

Appearance:
- Old hotel uniform
- Cracked golden mask
- Luggage filled with the voices of missing guests

Behavior:
- Does not sprint at first
- Knocks before entering
- Hunts harder when Saqer feels guilt
- Carries guests to rooms that confess their worst memories

Signature:
> Knock. Knock. Knock.  
> “Housekeeping.”

---

## 7. The Qareen

The Qareen is Saqer’s shadow-self.

It grows when the player:
- Uses excessive violence
- Kills when escape was possible
- Uses cruel bait
- Trusts weapons more than caution

It copies player habits:
- Same weapon
- Same routes
- Same hiding spots
- Same shoe throws

Core horror:
> The hotel cannot own an innocent survivor, but it can own a monster.

---

## 8. Shoes / Footsteps System

Shoes are not comedy only. They represent:
- Identity
- Movement
- Ownership
- Fate
- Social class
- Spiritual footprint

Mechanics:
- Wearing shoes = faster, stronger kick, louder
- Barefoot = quiet, but the hotel senses fear through the floor
- Throwing shoes = distraction, switch activation, enemy stun
- Losing a shoe = the hotel owns part of your path
- Keeping original shoe = required for the true ending

Key line:
> “My path is not yours to record.”

---

## 9. Choice Paths

The hotel records player behavior in hidden paths.

### Survival Way
Caution, restraint, refusing contracts, using weapons defensively.

### Debt Path
Signing papers, accepting shortcuts, using hotel help.

### Violence Path
Brutal fighting, unnecessary kills, cruel choices.

### Vision Path
Overusing Zarqa’s visions and trusting every future.

### Sacrifice Path
Saving souls, returning items, refusing selfish outcomes.

### Qareen Growth
A consequence of Violence Path and panic choices.

## 10. Chapter Scenario and Cutscenes

---

# CHAPTER 1: WELCOME BACK

### Opening Cutscene: The Emergency Receipt

Night. Saqer receives a message from an abandoned resort.

A machine prints:

> Guest Name: Saqer  
> Age: 18  
> Room: 000  
> Guardian: Not Required  
> Balance: One Unwritten Future  
> Collection Status: Active

The hotel speaker says:

> “Congratulations, Mr. Saqer. You are now old enough to pay.”

### First Choice
The check-in receipt requests a signature.

Choices:
1. **Sign**  
   Debt increases. The lobby opens easily. The hotel can call Saqer by name.
2. **Refuse**  
   Survival increases. Doors resist, but the hotel has weaker authority.
3. **Tear it**  
   Bellboy arrives earlier. Secret path opens.
4. **Burn it**  
   Survival increases strongly. Al-Mudawwin marks Saqer non-compliant.

---

# CHAPTER 2: THE CAMERAS WITH BLUE EYES

### Cutscene: CCTV Awakening

Every monitor shows Saqer from a different angle. Then:
- Saqer as a child
- Saqer old
- Saqer dead
- Saqer standing behind himself

Zarqa whispers:

> “I saw armies before they arrived.  
> I saw cities before they burned.  
> But you…  
> You are empty.”

### Vision Choice
Zarqa shows Saqer being dragged into Room 414.

Choices:
1. Follow vision exactly
2. Do the opposite
3. Study it and change one detail
4. Destroy the camera

Best survival path: study and change one detail.

---

# CHAPTER 3: ROOMS THAT CONFESS

### Room 101: The Missing Child’s Shoe
A child traded away by her mother’s ambition asks for her missing shoe.

Choices:
1. Keep the shoe
2. Throw it often
3. Return it
4. Use it as cruel bait

Best path: return it.

### Room 204: The Man Who Bought Silence
A businessman erased his brother from family memory.

Gameplay: Read contracts in order; wrong order brings Bellboy closer.

### Room 309: The Singer With No Voice
Umm Al-Duwais appears as perfume, music, and beauty.

Rule:
> Do not follow the scent.

---

# CHAPTER 4: THE RED CORRIDOR

### Cutscene: The Wedding Floor
A warning appears:

> لا تمش فوق العهد بحذائك  
> Do not walk over the vow with your shoes.

Choices:
1. Keep shoes on
2. Remove shoes
3. Throw one shoe ahead
4. Run barefoot

Best survival path: remove shoes and walk slowly.

### Barefoot Bride
She attacks anyone wearing shoes in her ballroom.

She reveals:
> “Your father also begged for the dead to return. But he begged for you.”

---

# CHAPTER 5: THE GUEST WHO BECAME YOU

### Cutscene: The Faceless Survivor
A survivor says:

> “The hotel cannot own what it cannot identify.”

Then Saqer’s shadow moves one second late.

The Qareen awakens.

Choices:
1. Follow survivor
2. Attack him
3. Question him
4. Watch his shadow

Best survival path: question him and watch the shadow.

---

# CHAPTER 6: THE BASEMENT BANK

### Cutscene: Memory ATM
The basement is a private bank where machines dispense memories.

Receipts read:
> Withdrawal: Mother’s voice  
> Deposit: Childhood fear  
> Transfer: Brother’s name  
> Balance Remaining: 3 years

The ATM asks:
> Enter the year you stopped believing him.

Correct answer: the year Saqer’s father disappeared.

The machine prints:

> Deposit: My life  
> Withdrawal: My son’s freedom  
> Status: Rejected  
> Reason: Son has no visible future

---

# CHAPTER 7: ROOM 000

### Cutscene: Father’s Room
Room 000 is Saqer’s childhood home.

Saqer finds his father, half-human and half-hotel record.

Father says:

> “Every year, I gave it one memory of you.  
> Your first word.  
> Your first step.  
> Your face at seven.  
> Your voice at twelve.  
> I gave everything I had of you, so it would not find you.”

Then he asks:

> “Are you my son?”

### Father Choice
1. Sign to save father
2. Kill corrupted father
3. Leave father and refuse contract
4. Promise to break the ledger

Survival path requires refusing the contract.

---

# FINAL CHAPTER: THE EYE TOWER

### Cutscene: Buried Upside-Down Tower
The player descends into the ancient tower. CCTV lenses grow from walls like insects. Zarqa’s eyeless body sits under blue cloth.

Zarqa says:

> “I saw every guest before they died.  
> But when I looked at you, there was nothing.”

Al-Mudawwin appears as ink, paper, contracts, and moving calligraphy.

It says:

> “A man without a written future is theft from reality.”

---

## Endings

### Debt Ending
Saqer signs or accepts too much help.

Final receipt:
> Balance transferred to next bloodline.

### Qareen Ending
Saqer uses violence too much.

Final line:
> “Housekeeping.”

### Eye Ending
Saqer gives his eyes to Zarqa.

Final line:
> “Do not fear the dark. I will see for you.”

### Survival Ending: The One Who Left
Saqer escapes through the maintenance tunnel. The hotel remains unresolved.

Final receipt:
> Guest escaped. Balance unresolved. Future unreadable.

### True Ending: The Unwritten Man
Saqer offers nothing.

He places his last shoe at the well:

> “My life is not a debt.  
> My father’s love was never a transaction.  
> My path is not yours to record.”

The contracts burn.

Final screen:
> CHECK-OUT COMPLETE  
> NO BALANCE REMAINING

---

## 11. Tone Rules

The horror must be:
- Psychological before physical
- Curious before confusing
- Tense before loud
- Symbolic but understandable
- Arabic in identity, global in emotional themes

Avoid:
- Too many monsters at once
- Random jump scares without story meaning
- Over-explaining the jinn
- Turning shoes into pure comedy
- Making Zarqa evil

Core player question:
> Am I surviving, or am I becoming something the hotel can own?
